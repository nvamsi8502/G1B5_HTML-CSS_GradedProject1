Body Pain

Painkillers: Ibuprofen, Acetaminophen.

Uses: Ibuprofen reduces inflammation, while Acetaminophen alleviates pain; both are effective for general body pain.

Symptoms: Muscle soreness, aches, and discomfort may be relieved with these medications.

Periods Pain:

Painkillers: NSAIDs (e.g., Ibuprofen), Acetaminophen.

Uses: NSAIDs reduce prostaglandins, easing menstrual pain, while Acetaminophen offers general pain relief.

Symptoms: Menstrual cramps and discomfort can be mitigated with these medications.

Acidity

Painkillers: Antacids (e.g., Tums), H2 blockers (e.g., Ranitidine).

Uses: Antacids neutralize stomach acid, H2 blockers reduce acid production, addressing acidity symptoms.

Symptoms: Menstrual cramps and discomfort can be mitigated with these medications.

Migraine Pain:

Painkillers: Triptans (e.g., Sumatriptan), NSAIDs.

Uses: Triptans target migraine-specific pathways, NSAIDs reduce pain and inflammation during a migraine.

Symptoms: Severe headache, nausea, light sensitivity; these medications target migraine symptoms.

Sinus and Headache:

Painkillers: Ibuprofen, Acetaminophen.

Uses: Ibuprofen eases sinus inflammation and pain, while Acetaminophen provides relief from headache symptoms

Symptoms: Sinus congestion, headache, and facial pain can be addressed with these medications.
